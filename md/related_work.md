# Related Work

## Traditional Machine Learning Methods

Machine learning has become an important tool for disease prediction by enabling the analysis of clinical and laboratory data to support early diagnosis. Several studies have compared multiple supervised learning algorithms to identify the most effective model for disease prediction. In a study on breast cancer recurrence prediction, eleven machine learning algorithms, including Logistic Regression, Random Forest, AdaBoost, XGBoost, and LightGBM, were evaluated using clinical and blood test data collected from 342 patients. The dataset was divided into training and testing sets, and three-fold cross-validation was applied to improve model reliability. Among all the models, AdaBoost achieved the best performance with an AUC of 0.987 and an accuracy of 97.1%. The study also applied SHAP analysis to identify the most influential clinical features contributing to recurrence prediction. Although the model demonstrated excellent predictive performance, the authors acknowledged limitations such as the relatively small sample size, single-center data collection, and the absence of genetic information, indicating the need for further validation on larger and more diverse populations.

Another study applied machine learning techniques for predicting type 2 diabetes subtypes using reproducible prediction models. The research demonstrated that machine learning methods can effectively classify patients into clinically meaningful diabetes subgroups using routinely collected healthcare data. The study emphasized reproducibility, robustness, and consistent model evaluation, showing that carefully designed machine learning models can improve disease stratification and support personalized treatment planning. These findings highlight the growing role of traditional machine learning techniques in supporting clinical decision-making across different medical applications.

## Deep Learning Methods

Deep learning techniques have increasingly been adopted for medical diagnosis because of their ability to automatically learn complex patterns from clinical data. One of the reviewed studies proposed a disease prediction framework that combined deep learning and ensemble learning methods. The pipeline included data collection, dataset preprocessing, feature extraction, missing value processing, model selection, and performance evaluation. Deep learning models such as Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) were combined with powerful machine learning algorithms including XGBoost and LightGBM to improve predictive performance. The study evaluated the models using cross-validation, accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, ROC curves, confusion matrix analysis, and SHAP-based explainability. The integration of deep learning with ensemble techniques demonstrated improved prediction accuracy while maintaining model interpretability, making the proposed framework suitable for practical healthcare applications.

## Adaptive Methods

Adaptive methods represent an emerging direction in artificial intelligence for healthcare by developing models that can support continuous learning and improved clinical decision-making. The reviewed literature on chronic kidney disease highlights the importance of early diagnosis because CKD is a progressive disease that often remains undetected until significant kidney damage has occurred. Accurate prediction systems can assist clinicians in identifying high-risk patients at earlier stages and improve treatment planning.

Another reviewed study examined the relationship between chronic kidney disease and valvular heart disease, explaining that chronic inflammation, vascular calcification, and metabolic disorders contribute to disease progression and increase patient morbidity and mortality. The authors emphasized the importance of timely diagnosis and multidisciplinary clinical management. These findings support the need for intelligent predictive systems capable of adapting to complex clinical conditions and providing reliable decision support. Adaptive AI methods have the potential to improve long-term prediction performance by incorporating updated patient information and maintaining robust performance in changing healthcare environments.

## Research Gap

The reviewed studies demonstrate that traditional machine learning, deep learning, and emerging adaptive approaches have significantly improved disease prediction performance. However, several limitations remain. Many existing studies rely on data collected from a single institution, reducing model generalizability across diverse populations. Some models primarily focus on maximizing prediction accuracy without extensive evaluation of robustness and real-world applicability. In addition, adaptive learning strategies capable of continuously improving model performance using new patient data remain relatively underexplored. Therefore, this project focuses on developing and evaluating an optimized Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) model for chronic kidney disease prediction using comprehensive performance evaluation, including accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, ROC-AUC, confusion matrix analysis, and cross-validation, to provide a reliable framework for proactive disease detection.
