### What to Replicate

After reading the paper, I found several techniques that can be applied to our project. The authors compared multiple machine learning and deep learning models instead of relying on a single algorithm. They also performed proper preprocessing, handled missing values, and evaluated models using multiple metrics such as Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-Score, and ROC-AUC. Another useful aspect is the use of explainable AI techniques such as SHAP to understand which features contribute most to the predictions. These practices can help improve the reliability and interpretability of our disease detection system.

### What to Improve

While the paper focuses on disease classification using laboratory test results, our project can be improved by focusing more on proactive disease detection. Instead of identifying diseases after symptoms become clear, we aim to predict disease risk at an earlier stage. Additional improvements include better handling of class imbalance, more extensive hyperparameter tuning, and experimenting with advanced neural network architectures. We can also develop a real-time prediction system that provides early risk alerts and supports healthcare decision-making.
